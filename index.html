GOALS

Fritzie Dizon is a woman of vision, heart, and drive. Her goals are rooted in self-improvement, compassion, and service. Every day, she strives to be her best self — mentally, emotionally, and physically — believing that growth and balance are essential for both personal fulfillment and meaningful impact. As a devoted dog mom, one of her goals is to provide a loving and enriching life for her furry companion. She dreams of owning a house with a spacious yard where her current dog and a future second pup can run freely and happily.

Fritzie also aims to give more of her time and energy to volunteering. She understands the importance of community support and seeks to play a greater role in helping others through consistent, hands-on service. Traveling is another aspiration, as she believes that exploring different cultures and perspectives enriches both the mind and spirit. Ultimately, Fritzie’s long-term professional goal is to become the president of a nonprofit foundation — a role where she can combine her leadership skills, nonprofit expertise, and deep desire to create lasting social change.

PERSONAL ACCOMPLISHMENTS

Fritzie's accomplishments reflect both determination and a genuine passion for meaningful work. She holds a Bachelor of Science in Environmental Analysis and Design from the University of California, Irvine, and a Master of Science in Nonprofit Management from Columbia University. These academic achievements laid the foundation for a career dedicated to service and impact.

Over the years, Fritzie has become a leading figure in fundraising and development for universities and research institutions — a role that requires strategic insight, strong relationship-building, and unwavering commitment. Rising to the top of her field is a testament to her expertise and perseverance. In addition to her professional success, Fritzie takes pride in having spent ten years living in New York City, a place known for its challenges and intensity. Surviving — and thriving — in such a demanding environment is no small feat, and it speaks to her resilience and adaptability.
